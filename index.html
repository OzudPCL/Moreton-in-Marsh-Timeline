Launch of consultation. Four-week period consultation with the public. All households sent a postcard in the area alongside lamppost wraps in the High Street and train station.

Community Drop-in Event

Community drop-in event held at Redesdale Hall on 25th June to gather further feedback and discuss specific issues and solutions from the community.

Early Feedback Analysis

Analysis of consultation responses and detailed feedback review to be undertaken. Key themes, concerns and opportunities are identified to help determine whether there is support for changes and what types of measures may be appropriate for the town.

August 2026
Upcoming

Report on Early Feedback

Final report submitted, setting out findings, consultation outcomes, technical assessments and recommendations for next steps.

Autumn 2026
Upcoming

Development of Options

Using the evidence gathered through the feasibility study and consultation, a series of potential options and recommendations are developed. These may include measures such as resident parking schemes, short-stay and long-stay parking management, changes to parking restrictions, improved disabled parking provision, electric vehicle charging infrastructure, cycle parking facilities and other transport improvements.

Late 2026
Upcoming

Statutory Consultation Period

Detailed designs will be prepared for the preferred option that best represents the community’s demands. A statutory consultation will be undertaken to confirm the final designs and make any modifications or mitigations in response to feedback from the community.

2027
Upcoming

Implementation

Subject to consultation outcomes, funding availability, and any required approvals, agreed improvements will be implemented. Construction will begin shortly after with the community kept up to date on when changes will take effect.
